Cassava

Cassava (Manihot esculenta) is a tropical root vegetable and a staple crop in many parts of the world, especially in Africa, Asia, and Latin America. It is also known by various other names, including yuca (not to be confused with yucca, a different plant), manioc, and tapioca. Cassava is a major source of carbohydrates and provides essential nutrients for millions of people in regions with suitable climates.

Cashew

Cashew plants are tropical evergreen trees that belong to the genus Anacardium and are primarily grown for their cashew nuts and cashew apples.Cashew plants are native to northeastern Brazil but are now grown in many tropical regions of Asia.The most well-known part of the cashew plant is the cashew nut. The cashew nut is actually the seed of the cashew apple, which is a fleshy, pear-shaped fruit. The nut is found attached to the bottom of the cashew apple.
