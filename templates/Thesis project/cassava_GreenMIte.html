Cassava green mite Disease

Cassava green mite

Cassava green mite disease, also known as cassava green mite infestation, is not caused by A disease is rather caused by a specific pest. This pest is commonly called "cassava green". mite” or “Mononichelus tanazoa.” These mites are small arachnids that feed on cassava plants (manihot) and can damage leaves, reducing plant vigor and potentially affecting crop yield.
Here are some key features and effects of cassava green mite infestation:
Mite Appearance: Cassava green mite is very small, usually less than 0.5 mm. they are Cassava leaves have a green color, which can make them difficult to detect.
Symptoms: Early symptoms of cassava green mite include stippling and yellowing. Cassava leaves. Mites pierce plant cells and feed by sucking the cell contents, which grow Small, yellow or white spots (stippling) on ​​the upper part of the leaves. Leaves drop as the infestation progresses May turn yellow or bronze, and in severe cases, leaves may drop.
Impact: A heavy infestation of cassava green mite can reduce photosynthesis and growth, leading to low cassava yields. In severe cases, rotting can occur, further compromising plant health.
Spread: Cassava green mites can be spread by wind dispersal, carried on clothing, Equipment, or animals, and by movement of infected plant material.
Here are some acaricides (miticides) that can be considered for cassava green mite management:
Abamectin, Fenazaquin, Bifenazate, Spiromesifen.
Biological Control: Can be introduced as predatory mites and insects that feed on cassava green mites. Natural enemies help control pests.
Cultural Practices: Proper sanitation including removal and destruction of infected plant material, Can reduce the source of mites. Maintain good field hygiene and prevent spread of infection Essential from one area to another.
Resistant Varieties: Some cassava varieties may show resistance to cassava green mite and plant hopper These varieties can reduce the risk of infection.
Cassava green mite can be a significant concern for cassava growers and monitoring cassava is essential Regular plants for signs of infection. Early detection and appropriate management strategies can help Minimize the impact of cassava green mite infestation on crop productivity.
