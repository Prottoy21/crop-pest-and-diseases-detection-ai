Cashew Anthracnose Disease

Cashew Anthracnose

Anthracnose is a common fungal disease that can affect a variety of trees, including cashew Tree (Anacardium occidental). It is caused by various species of fungi of the genus Colletotrichum.
Here are some key features and effects of anthracnose disease on cashew trees:
Foliar symptoms: Anthracnose on cashew leaves usually appears as small, dark, sunken lesions Reddish-brown to black in color. These lesions may have a concentric ring pattern and may expand, resulting in In severe cases infected leaves may drop prematurely which affects the overall health of the plant.
Stem symptoms: Anthracnose can also affect cashew stems, causing cankers or lesions. this Can girdle the stem, leading to loss of branches or even the entire tree if the infection is severe.
Cashew Nut Symptoms: Anthracnose can affect cashew nuts in some cases. It can be dark, drowning Bruises or blemishes on the surface of the nuts, making them unmarketable or unfit for consumption.
Symptoms of cashew apples: Anthracnose can cause cashew apples to rot and wilt its quality.
Spread: Anthracnose is spread by fungal spores, which can be carried by wind, rain or Contact with infected plant material.
Management of anthracnose in cashew trees usually involves cultural, chemical and preventive measures, including:
Mancozeb, Copper-Based Fungicides, Chlorothalonil, Azoxystrobin, Propiconazole, Tebuconazole
1. Pruning and removing infected plant parts to reduce disease spread.
2. Apply fungicide to prevent further infection.
3. Provide proper spacing and ventilation in cashew plantations to reduce humidity and create less favorable conditions
4. Conditions for Fungal Growth.
5. Practicing good garden sanitation by removing and destroying infected plant debris.
6. Selecting cashew varieties that can be resistant to anthracnose.
7. Effective disease management strategies can help reduce the impact of anthracnose on cashew trees and Ensuring healthy yields for cashew farmers.
